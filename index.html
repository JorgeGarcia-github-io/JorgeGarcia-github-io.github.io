Best cars from the 60´s

During the 60’s; American car companies dominated the field. General Motors, Ford and Chrysler had a huge market in America; and they also sold many cars overseas. Races were becoming mainstream, and customers started to look for increased speed and performance. Safety features such as seatbelts and power steering made their way into manufacturing too.

Top Cars

Ford GT40: The GtT40 is a 24h of LeMans legend. It was born in order to compete with Ferrari, that had won in LeMans for the last six years. The car was developed by the newborn Ford Racing team along with Carroll Shelby ; the motorsport legend. It has been remade twice in the 21st century.

Lamborghini Miura: The Miura is known to be the first real supercar of all time. Its iconic v12 engine block has been used by Lamborghini for decades, even in modern cars such as the Countach. It has 350 HP; and it had the highest top speed at the time.

Ferrari 250 GTO: The 250 GTO is probably the best Ferrari roadcar from the 60’s. Only 36 were made between 1962 and 1964. It can reach up to 280 km/h . In 2018, one of these was sold for 80 million dollars. This price makes it the most expensive car ever sold on an auction.

Shelby Cobra: In order to develop the Cobra, Caroll Shelby focused on lowering the weight and increasing the power. Although it is quite small, it fits a V8 under its hood that produces 410 HP. It can go from 0-100km/h in 4.2 seconds, and its unique silhouette is an emblem of car culture.

Best cars from the 70´s

In the 70’s; the American car industry took a mayor hit. Sales suffered and foreign cars took over the American market. In Europe, compact cars became hugely popular. Fuel economy started to matter due to the gas crisis. Japan imported many cars to Europe and America, making companies such as Toyota and Honda grow tons.

Top Cars

Dodge Challenger: The first Challenger model made its debut in 1970, and new models are still being produced each year. This particular model is a particulary iconic muscle car; and its big hood and straight angles are still present in modern Challengers

Porsche 930 Turbo: This car is based on the 911, but it features the first turbocharger ever applied to a 911. It has a wider body in order to fit the wider rear tires and a huge spoiler. Although it was pretty fast, it had a tendency to brutally oversteer mid-corner. This problem is common in rear-wheel drive cars, although it isn’t as intense.

Lancia Stratos: The Stratos is a very important rally icon. Despite being designed for rally races; the FIA forced each company to build 500 road legal units from each car in order to compete. Although its engine isn’t very powerful; it can reach 200km/h in almost every surface.

BMW M1: The M1 is the only official supercar that BMW has made. It has some unique features such as its mid-engine and its fibre glass body. BMW M,BMW´s performance branch developed the whole thing, and the machine was built between German and Italian constructors; which wasn´t common back then.

Best cars from the 80´s

The 80’s were a controversial decade that made a significant change in technology, designs and classification of cars. Due to tight emissions and environmental laws, the power figures weren´t high. In spite of this, manufacturers found out how to produce powerful cars using turbo technology. The 80’s car imports were dominated by Japanese made cars; that were famous for their reliability and low price.

Top Cars

Ferrari F40: Many people believe that the F40 is the best Ferrari of all time. That’s because it was very ahead of its time. It has a ultra-light carbon fiber body along with a powerful V8 that is paired with two twin turbos. The F40 was the first production car that could reach 320 km/h, and its big spoiler and pop-up headlights are an icon of the 80´s.
